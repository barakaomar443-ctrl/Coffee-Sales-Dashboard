# Coffee-Sales-Dashboard
This project presents an interactive **Coffee Sales Dashboard** built with Microsoft Excel. It analyzes sales data from a coffee shop chain to uncover trends, identify top customers, and track performance across different countries and coffee types. The dashboard enables stakeholders to make data-driven decisions regarding inventory, marketing, and customer engagement.

## Objectives
- Track total sales over time to identify seasonal patterns.
- Compare sales performance across different countries.
- Identify top 5 customers by revenue.
- Analyze sales by coffee type (Arabica, Excelsa, Liberica, Robusta).
- Provide an interactive tool for quick business insights.

## Process
- started by gathering the raw data from an Excel file that contained three separate sheets: one for customer details, one for product information, and one with a record of every order placed.

- cleaned and prepared the data by removing any duplicate entries, making sure all dates and numbers were in the correct format, and creating new columns like "Year" and "Month" to make the time-based analysis easier.

- connected the three tables together so that every order was linked to the correct customer name, country, loyalty card status, and specific product details like coffee type and size.

- With the complete dataset ready, I built an interactive dashboard in Excel using charts like line graphs for sales over time and bar charts for sales by country and top customers, adding slicers so the whole view updates with a single click.

- Exploring the dashboard, I discovered key insights such as the United States being the dominant market, the top five customers, and noticeable sales peaks during certain months, and then I uploaded the entire project to GitHub for others to see.

## Project insights

-Market Concentration: The United States is the dominant market, contributing ~78% of total sales among the three countries ($35,639 out of $45,135). Ireland and the United Kingdom follow with significantly lower shares of $6,697 and $2,799 respectively.

-Top Customers Contribution: The top 5 customers collectively generate $1,473 in sales, with Allis Wilmore leading at $317. This small customer segment represents a valuable base for loyalty programs and targeted marketing.

-Seasonal Trend: Sales show noticeable peaks in September 2021 (over $800) and February 2020, with fluctuations throughout the year. This pattern suggests specific months drive higher sales and could be leveraged for promotions.

-Product Variety: The dashboard tracks four coffee types (Arabica, Excelsa, Liberica, Robusta) across multiple roast types and sizes, enabling stakeholders to identify best-selling products and optimize inventory decisions.

-Loyalty Program Potential: Customer data includes loyalty card information, allowing analysis of whether loyalty members purchase more frequently or have higher order values than non-members.

## Final Conclusion

The coffee business shows strong dependency on the US market and a small group of top customers. While this concentration drives current revenue, it also presents a risk. To sustain growth, the company should:

Strengthen its presence in the dominant US market while exploring growth opportunities in Ireland and the UK through targeted marketing campaigns.

Develop loyalty programs or exclusive offers for top customers like Allis Wilmore and Brenn Dundredge to retain their business and encourage repeat purchases.

Leverage peak sales months (September and February) with seasonal promotions and discounts to maximize revenue during high-demand periods.

Analyze underperforming coffee types or roast preferences to optimize product mix and reduce inventory costs.

Use customer insights to tailor email marketing and improve customer experience for both loyalty card holders and regular buyers.
